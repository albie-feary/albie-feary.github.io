Connecting

Growing trust and sincerity

Certainly, I've used these techniques naturally in most things I do. I am contantly thrown into happiness with acts of kindess. Kindness is cool. And as Mr Meng states, "Empathy comes pre installed in our brains." Kindess is the engine of empathy and is the engine of sustainable happiness. Empathy builds trust which is the foundation of all effective teams. And trust in vulnerability based situations such as our very own Dev Bootcamp is what I suspects makes the special EDA culture tick. No one member of a team is then looking inward to save their ego, rather trusting the intentions of the group and allow themselves to be vulnerable - sending the group in a radical direction together. I'll be looking to embrace this experience trust in a vulnerable state and so enter bootcamp with an open mind ready to ask 1000 silly questions without my ego hinering me.

“Whatever one frequently thinks and ponders upon, that will become the inclination of his mind.” (Whatever we think, we become.) Buddha

Empathic listening

Listening with empathy is similar to mindfull listening but with kindess added. This is hugely satisfing as once again it takes the practice of mindfullness off the mat and into the everyday grind. This is something I intend to implement more as human connection through great conversation is a wonderful thing.

Loving kindness meditation

This is a beautiful exercise in its magic to send out positive vibes out there. There has to be power in that. And sure enough, thanks to Mr Meng, there is science to back it. Kindess for self and all sentient beings (my dog included) radically improves happiness, which is good for business and well, life of course.

Political awareness exercise

This exercise in political awareness (which takes the needs of a person and weaves it into the organisation as a whole) created the ability to look at an otherwise complex situation and regard it with more clarity and objectivity. I tend to think I am intuitively good at this practice but formulating the words to describe such circumstances is difficult.
