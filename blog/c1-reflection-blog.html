Shereef's Dev Academy Chat

23 Feb 17

What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

The DBC/EDA experience to me is an incredibly exciting opportunity! Its an experience unlike any other I've ever had. What strikes me as so important is EDA's culture of learning whereby we are pushed forward to be in a 'vulnerable state' in order to accelerate learning and create a closer, more empathetic cohort. I like that it's incredibly diverse and core values of EDA is engineering with empathy, kindness and integrity. "You are who your surround yourslef with" is something I identify with and this seems to be one EDA's strengths. Take people from all walks of life, with a passion for tech and something magical can happen when everyone puts their best efforts in.

What are your impressions?

I am incredibly impressed with how open and supportive my cohort team has been already (and its only day four.) I am aware the workload is going to get more and more complex so it is important to keep up and understand concepts. I am however enjoying those little wins and 'aha' moments!

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

Engaging with this kind of culture requires kindness, integrity and empathy. I also intend on engaging with this culture by speaking up when I dont understand and making full use of the resources available.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

Expectations have definitely changed but only for the better! I feel like they will be constantly changing from week to week as we progress onwards. My biggest expectation shift so far is that its totally ok to ask any niggly questions and not even feel silly about it as we are all in this together.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I'm incredibly excited and of course nervous all at once. My biggest challenge currently is juggling phase 0 sprints with work commitments! I would love to spend more time on tasks and gain deeper understanding without worrying about not making finishing the tasks in time. This is something that I will constantly monitor and reflect upon.
